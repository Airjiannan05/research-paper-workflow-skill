# Rebuttal and Revision Ledger

Use this for reviewer response, revision planning, and resubmission strategy. Do not invent new results or claim experiments are completed unless the user supplied them.

## Review issue taxonomy

- misunderstanding caused by writing;
- missing experiment or baseline;
- weak novelty positioning;
- method clarity gap;
- evaluation validity concern;
- citation/related-work gap;
- limitation or ethics concern;
- impossible/out-of-scope request;
- factual reviewer error.

## Response strategy

For each reviewer comment:

1. Restate the concern neutrally.
2. Decide whether to concede, clarify, rebut with evidence, or promise a bounded revision.
3. Link the response to a manuscript change, experiment, citation, or limitation.
4. Avoid emotional language and overclaiming.
5. Track the action in the revision ledger.

## Revision ledger schema

```text
Comment ID:
Reviewer:
Concern type:
Severity:
Response stance: concede | clarify | rebut | partial | out-of-scope
Evidence available:
Manuscript action:
Experiment action:
Citation action:
Status: open | planned | done | cannot-do
Response draft:
```

## Rebuttal style

- Start with a short thanks + high-level summary of changes.
- Prioritize fatal concerns first.
- Use concrete evidence, not generic reassurance.
- If results are pending, say they are pending.
- Do not bury concessions; make the repair credible.

## Resubmission plan

After rejection or major revision, produce:

```text
Fatal issues:
Salvageable strengths:
Must-run experiments:
Writing/positioning repairs:
Venue retargeting options:
Timeline and risk:
Revision ledger:
```
